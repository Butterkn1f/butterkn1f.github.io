About

VTubers (ブイチューバー), or Virtual YouTubers, are online entertainers who use a virtual avatar generated using computer graphics and motion capture technology.

Virtual Avatar

Virtual Avatar

The traditional facecam is replaced with a virtual avatar that tracks the facial movement and expressions of the person behind the camera.

Their personalities vary widely, and while some accurately portray whatever character their avatar is, some have personalities that lean more towards themselves in real life.

Identity

Identity

VTubers are not bound by physical limitations, and many of them engage in activities that are unconstrained by their real-world identity.

They are free to express themselves and talk about topics that they are otherwise too shy to talk about in real life, which is one of the major appeals of them.

Japan

Japan

VTubers originated from Japan, as such most of these virtual avatars are in the art style of anime.

Because of this, its original and largest percentage of its fanbase is part of the anime community as well.

Before English VTubers became popular, fans who understood both languages helped to translate clips of the Japanese streams into English subtitles, thus appealing to an even larger audience.

Content

Content

Similar to other content creators on the platform, their content can be extremely diverse.

They cover existing songs, or even sing original made ones of their own, accompanied by their own music videos.

They also livestream, which can be about gaming, karaoke streams, drawing, or even cooking. Anything goes!

Agencies

Agencies

VTubers can be indepedently produced or employed by talent agencies. The majority of popular VTubers today are part of such talent agencies.

Streamers are employed by an agency to portray characters developed by the company, which are then commercialized via merchandising and other promotional appearances, as well as traditional revenue streams such as monetization of their videos, and viewer donations.

The more popular agencies, such as hololive and NIJISANJI, even hold auditions for the public to get a chance to be employed by them!

Platform

Platform

Despite their name, VTubers are not exclusive to YouTube, and are popular on various other platforms as well, such as Twitch or BiliBili.

There are even entire agencies that operate entirely outside of YouTube, such as VShojo.
